Approach

To make urban mobility data more accessible and provide urbanists with the tools necessary to examine trends in transportation, Citibike has made their data public. With this information, I decided to analyze Citibike data from May 2020, one of the first few months of the COVID-19 pandemic. I was interested to see users’ behavior during Jersey City’s period of lockdown, with many businesses shuttered during this initial outbreak.

 

Throughout this analysis, my goal was to gather some initial insights into Citibike use, including descriptive information and visualization, using origin-destination analysis, to understand this data a bit better. Moving forward, I would like to compare this month to a May 2019 and May 2021, a year before and after COVID-19 shut down the city, to better assess the impact of the pandemic on bike usage.

 

Descriptive Analysis


To assess an overall pattern of Ctiibike usage throughout this month, I first decided to plot the Citbike Trips per day in May 2020. I found that this data varied widely throughout the month spiking initially within the fist five days of May, then again in the middle od the month around May 15th, and then exhibiting two separate, later peaks around May 25th and May 30th.

It would be interesting, in further analysis to consider social, economic, and political trends that occurred during these peaks. One potential reason for an increase in Citbike usage across the city could be the wave of anti-police violence protests during this time.

 

Origin-Destination Analysis

 

I then proceeded with an origin-destination (OD) analysis to locate patterns in travel throughout this month-long period. To do so, I first had to extract the nodes (i.e., the Citibike stations), to create the origin and destination of all the trips taken throughout May 2020. Through this process, I identified 58 total stations in Jersey City. I created a data frame using this node dictionary that included the name, latitude, longitude, trips to and trips from which I later used to construct my visualization.

 

I then determined the top 10 Citibike stations that experienced the most incoming and outgoing rides, to better understand which Citbike stations riders were journeying to and from the most.

 

 

Overwhelmingly, the Liberty Light Rail station was the most utilized, with 2415 trips to and 2380 trips from said station. Positioned next to Liberty State Park and the historic downtown area of Jersey City, this makes sense. The trips to and the trips from, for nearly all of the top 10 stations align sequentially.

 

 

Centrality

 

The next step in my analysis, was to consider measures of centrality. The first measure, degree centrality, considers the sheer number of links attached to a given node. The second, eigenvector centrality, examines how “influential” a node is, i.e., how many other highly valued nodes it’s connected with.

 

Degree Centrality

 

When considering degree centrality, the Liberty Light Rail station remains the highest. While the top 10 stations experiencing incoming and outgoing trips are not entirely aligned, there is still significant overlap. The Grove St Path station and the Newport Pkwy remain in the top 5. The Hamilton Park station rises from 6th to 2nd, reflecting its significant number of links.

 

 

 

 

 

Eigenvector Centrality

 

 

When considering eigenvector centrality, there remains some overlap, however the Columbus Dr at Exchange Pl station climbs to the number one spot, evidence of how influential it is in terms of connections to other highly important nodes.
